Details

In 2022, four healthcare personnel design workshops were conducted. Before each workshop, I organized previous interview data and created Personas and Scenarios for five elderly individuals and seven caregivers. In total, approximately 70 participants attended the workshops. We generated Stakeholder Relationship Maps, How Might We statements, and design concepts. Clinical experiences from medical professionals were also integrated into the Personas and Scenarios.

After the workshops, following the model of a Singaporean design team, we analyzed the workshop outcomes and published them in a design workshop handbook.
Additionally, I took charge of organizing and analyzing the data, with a particular focus on reinterpreting the How Might We data to explore healthcare-related issues. This analysis process and its results were compiled into a conference paper, and I presented it at a medical conference in June 2023.
We also turned the design concepts from the workshops into prototypes and invited clinical healthcare personnel to participate in focus group interviews to assess the feasibility of the designs.
This year, we expanded our fieldwork to include day care and long-term care centers. We conducted field observations at an institution in Tainan and created Personas and a Service Blueprint for the facility. A workshop was subsequently held with 15 participants. This workshop produced Stakeholder Relationship Maps, How Might We statements, and design concepts, supplemented by data from the field observations. Currently, the team is in the process of analyzing the data and collaborating with the institution to proceed with prototype design.

During my tenure as a research assistant, I also assisted in conducting usability tests for a LINEBot in one of the subprojects. After the testing, we redesigned it with a user-centric approach, resulting in new usage scenarios and interfaces.
