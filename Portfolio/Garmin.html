Details

This is a three-month internship where my mentor provided a Design Brief on Mental Health. I conducted desktop research, user research, market analysis, and design ideation based on this Design Brief. Initially, I extensively read literature and articles on mental health from both domestic and international sources to understand the background of mental health. I aimed to discover the relationship between mental health and physical activity, resulting in the research goal: exploring methods of self-care and daily mental health activities. I used this goal as a foundation for more in-depth research.
I delved into the market to understand the services offered by competitors in the mental health domain. I analyzed the interaction methods, content, and more in these services. I ultimately analyzed three wearable devices and ten self-care software products.
Furthermore, I interviewed four users who had used mental health products and had some mental health conditions, such as academic or work-related frustrations, stress from social interactions, life changes, etc. These conditions made them experience feelings of depression, anxiety, lack of focus, and sleep disturbances. Additionally, I interviewed two counseling psychologists to understand clinical processes, professional knowledge, and shared my design proposals to confirm their feasibility.
Through market analysis and user research, I found that current mental health products include initial assessments, the development of self-care functions, daily self-care recommendations, self-care programs, and user condition assessments. Additionally, these products often employ positive reinforcement techniques to encourage continued usage.
After three months of the internship, I developed three proposals. These proposals include providing users with personalized mental health plans based on physiological data, indicators, and subjective experiences, designing music/sound to provide relaxation during self-care, and customizing self-care functions. All three proposals are designed for both mobile phones and wearable devices.
