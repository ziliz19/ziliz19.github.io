Details

I audited a User Experience and Usability Evaluation course at National Chiao Tung University from March 2020. This course involved collaboration with a company, and the project was carried out in partnership with Line Taxi (formerly Taxi Go). The approach to the project involved learning through the course, guidance from the instructor, and defining research problems, objectives, and methods from our perspective. We provided the company with our findings and design recommendations. The course was divided into six milestones, with two team members taking turns as project managers for each milestone. I served as the PM for Milestone 2 (User Research, Persona, Scenario) and Milestone 5 (Usability Testing).
Initially, we held meetings with the company to present our project timeline and understand the specific areas they wanted to explore. Ultimately, we decided to focus on the passenger's experience for this research project. After planning the project schedule, we met with the company to align our ideas and completed an interaction map, defining research questions.
To understand user engagement with LINE Taxi, we conducted interviews with five users to understand their booking process. We transcribed the interview data verbatim and performed qualitative data analysis using an Affinity Diagram. Based on the results, we established user behavior variables and created personas and scenarios.
Our competitive analysis focused on online ride-hailing platforms. We collected user experience data from various products, identified key features and additional functionalities, and selected nine competitors for in-depth analysis. This allowed us to identify features that LINE Taxi could learn from. We then conducted a data analysis combining user research and competitive analysis to highlight LINE Taxi's strengths and weaknesses.
Following this, we conducted a survey among users who had used LINE Taxi to investigate factors influencing their decision to continue or discontinue using the service. The survey was divided into three main sections: factors of concern for customers, issues customers encountered, and basic demographic information. We used Survey Cake to create and distribute the survey through LINE Taxi's official channels. We received 1,208 valid responses and conducted further analysis.
After completing the survey, we conducted a heuristic evaluation following Jakob Nielsen's guidelines to identify pain points in the user's journey during a ride. Each team member individually assessed the severity of each pain point, resulting in the identification of ten important findings.
In the final stage, we recruited participants through a Facebook group for usability testing of the tasks we designed. We recruited three pilot testers and seven formal testers. During usability testing, we conducted interviews, usability tasks, and collected user feedback through questionnaires and interviews. Ultimately, our research led to three major findings: fragmented chat messages, misalignment of chatbot mechanisms with user expectations, and limited potential for chatbot use in ride-hailing services.
