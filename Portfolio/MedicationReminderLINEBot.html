Details

This course was co-taught by two instructors from the Industrial Design Department and the Electrical Engineering Department. Through interdisciplinary collaboration, it aimed to bring design-centered products to fruition and make engineering ideas more user-centric. Our team consisted of one designer and six engineers, and we conducted user research with a focus on medication issues for the elderly.
After completing the user research, I performed market and product analysis to understand the services provided by medication-related software in the market. This included analyzing their AOF and identifying the drawbacks of competitors and the aspects worth retaining.
We delved deep into the context of medication management and found that LINE is a platform frequently used by the elderly, with which they are familiar and comfortable compared to other platforms. Therefore, we chose LINE as the platform for medication reminders. We incorporated roles for both the elderly users and caregivers, allowing the elderly to remember to take their medications and enabling caregivers to stay informed about their medication status in real time. Caregivers can also access historical data, and in the future, the system could provide reference information to healthcare professionals.

The system was developed on the front end using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. For the back end and database, we utilized PHP and SQL. The integration with LINE was achieved through the LINE API, making the system accessible on the LINE platform.
