Details

Our team was responsible for the Re-design of the Forest and Land Recreation Website. We began by analyzing the website's information architecture, stakeholder maps, and benchmarking against similar competitors. We conducted a heuristic evaluation of the existing site and engaged in discussions with the Forestry Bureau and the original design firm.

We used surveys to gain insights into users' experiences with the Forest and Land Recreation Website. In total, we received 148 survey responses and conducted in-depth interviews with seven users to understand their usage experiences. Ultimately, we created two personas, user scenarios, and Jobs to Be Done (JTBD). We also generated a user journey map based on interview results, which can be divided into three stages: before, during, and after the trip. The "before" stage had the most data.

Through the interview data, we understood users' feelings and proposed two Chatbot concepts. One was a Chatbot built into the website to help users easily find information, and the other was designed to leverage LINE's market share in Taiwan, aiming to provide users with more information related to the Forestry Bureau through a LINEBot. After discussing with the Forestry Bureau, they expressed a strong preference for the LINEBot proposal. They had already been considering developing a LINEBot for the Forestry Bureau, so we proceeded to further design this concept using Figma to create both low-fidelity and high-fidelity prototypes.
On the day of the presentation, we prepared a presentation, demo video, posters, and prototype testing to ensure that visitors could fully understand our design and have a positive interactive experience with the prototype. Additionally, six months after the project's completion, the Forestry Bureau invited us to their office to share our design process and results with the director and other colleagues.
